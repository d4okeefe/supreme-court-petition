Cover Rule

Rule 34.1

CV Plain Language

The petition cover must have, in this sequence: docket number space, court name, parties caption, nature of the proceeding, document title, and filer’s information block.

Docket Number Form

Create a space for the Clerk’s Office to stamp your docket number. Common styles include

  • No.: ________
  • Docket No.: ________
  • 20-________

Docket Number Tip

Your petition probably does not have a docket number yet. If you filed an earlier application or motion in this case, the Clerk’s Office will assign a new docket number to the petition. If this is a Rule 12.5 conditional cross petition, the Clerk’s Office will assign a new docket number, linked to the underlying petition’s docket. You SHOULD ONLY INCLUDE A DOCKET NUMBER if you have already filed an IFP petition in this case (Rule 12.2 and Rule 33.2), but the Court denied your indigency application and gave you time to refile in booklet format; in that instance, you should type in the docket number that the Clerk’s Office assigned to the original IFP petition.

Docket Number Tip

If you use a docket number style that pre-prints the term-year prefix (like "20-"), note that the Court’s docketing cycle runs from the beginning of July through the end of June. So for example, petitions docketed between July 2020 and June 2021 will all receive docket numbers starting "20-," even those filed in the calendar year 2021.

Docket Number Tip

Do not include a line like, "October Term 2020." You may have seen that style on older petition examples, but the Court no longer wants this text on the cover.

Court Name Form

The standard style of this requirement reads, "In the Supreme Court of the United States".

Caption Guidance

Do not automatically use the same caption that appeared on documents in the courts below. A Supreme Court caption follows different standards, and your petition caption might be different. Take a moment to review this guidance about putting together a correct Supreme Court caption.

Caption: ATTENTION

If you do not list all petitioners and all respondents in the caption, you must include a parties listing in the index (Rule 14.1(b)(i)).

Nature of the Proceedings Form

Your petition will initiate the petition-stage of this case, so the proceedings line should begin, "On Petition for..." You are likely seeking a writ of certiorari (although Rule 20 describes other writ types), so the next clause should read, "...a Writ of Certiorari…" And the final clause should identify the last court that considered the merits of your case, saying, "...to the [full name of the last merits court]."

Nature of the Proceedings Tip

The last merits court may not be the last court to issue an order. This issue often arises when a state’s highest court declined to take discretionary review. If a lower court’s order essentially says, "We decided not to look at this…" it is likely not a merits order; but if it says, "We looked at this and decided... " then it is a merits order. You should make a fair attempt to identify the proper merits court, but understand that an incorrect identification will not disqualify your petition.

Document Title Form

You can use any title that identifies this as a petition. Common styles include

  • Petition
  • Petition for Writ of Certiorari
  • Petition for a Writ of Certiorari

Filer Information Block Plain Language

The cover must have the filer’s name, mailing address, telephone number, and email address (attorneys). If you list more than one attorney, you must designate a single attorney - who must be a member of the Supreme Court Bar - as the counsel of record.

Filer Information Block Guidance

Many different considerations can affect your filer information block. Review this guidance for more information about requirements and styles.

Filer Information Block Tip

If the counsel of record is not yet a member of the Supreme Court bar, but an application is pending and expected to be granted, you can list that attorney as counsel of record, and file the petition with an accompanying letter to the Clerk explaining the situation. The Clerk’s Office will note that the petition was timely-filed and hold the copies until the membership comes through.
