Questions Presented Rule

Rule 14.1(a)

QP Plain Language

In this required section, you should provide a short and concise presentation of the legal controversy that you are asking the Court to resolve with your petition, without getting too deeply into the specific circumstances of your case.

QP: ATTENTION

The Clerk’s Office might reject your petition if you include too much text before your first question. A short introductory statement is permitted, but Rule 14.1(a) says that the QP should “be set out on the first page following the cover,” and the Clerk’s Office might reject your petition if the first actual question does not at least begin on the first page.

QP Form

The QP can either be a true question, ending with a question mark, or a whether-statement, like, "Whether the lower court..."

Parties Listing Rule

Rule 14.1(b)(i)

PL: ATTENTION

If you did not list all petitioners and all respondents in the caption on the cover, you must include a parties listing in the index (Rule 14.1(b)(i)).

PL Tip

The PL page can be used to explain inconsistencies between a lower court caption and the caption of your petition. If a party has been substituted for another since the lower court proceedings (i.e., a new governor took office or a new warden appointed), if the parties were aligned differently below, or if you dropped a party because you believe that party has no interest in the outcome of the petition (see Rule 12.6), you can describe those circumstances in your PL.

Corporate Disclosure Rules

Rule 14.1(b)(ii)   &   Rule 29.6

CD Plain Language

If any petitioner is a non-governmental corporation, this is where you must make the disclosures required by Rule 29.6.

CD Form

Many filers opt to combine the PL and CD into a single section, with a heading that reads something like, “Parties to the Proceedings and Rule 29.6 Statement.”

CD Tip

If a petitioner’s identification on the cover or in the parties listing does not clearly demonstrate that the party is a NOT a non-governmental corporation, you can use the PL or the CD to clear up any ambiguity, something like, “XYZ, Ltd. is a sole proprietorship and not subject to disclosure under Rule 29.6.” This will help the Clerk’s Office determine that you have satisfied the Rules, and avoid possible docketing delays.

Statement of Related Cases Rule

Rule 14.1(b)(iii)

SORC Plain Language

This is a required section for virtually all petitions, because the term “related cases” includes the lower court proceedings in this case. If applicable, you should also list cases arising out of the same occurrence or circumstances, raising similar legal questions, but that were addressed in separate legal proceedings.

SORC Form

List each separate proceeding by identifying the court, the docket number and case caption in that court, and the judgment date.

SORC Tip

The information provided here is similar to the Opinions Below requirement in your main brief (Rule 14.1(d)), but you must include both sections.
